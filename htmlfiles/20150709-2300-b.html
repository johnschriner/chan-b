Uninitialized means there's no partition table. That means it's:

a) a new, blank hard drive (doesn't look like it);
b) been deliberately overwritten (erased); or
c) faulty or data has been corrupted

If you initialize, partition or format it, you'll be potentially overwriting old data that was there before, making its recovery less likely.

Use Testdisk (free) to quickly rewrite and recover any lost partitions or GetDataBack (available on The Pirate Bay) to do a deeper, slower search.

If you've tried to initialize it and it's failed, you've probably got yourself a faulty drive, which would explain why you found it.

Get a few general traits out there, regarding her mentality to relationships and her place in the world, and then decide whether she's adjusted or maladjusted, or a mix of both. When you figure out what she wants out of life, it's important to determine why she wants that, and not something else.

Work with some sort of continuum on her traits, where their expression is modified by her emotional state at the time. For instance, if it turns out that she enjoys domineering because she loves to be in control, she'll behave in one way when she's assured of control, and another way when she feels she's losing control, or has had control robbed from her.

The big step, though, is getting this personality fleshed out through interaction with other characters, which helps define her through action and interaction. Where there's a positive trait, there's probably also a flaw, and flaws are also fun to work with -- lots of interesting things can happen in a scene when the action draws out character flaws, or even just idiosyncrasies that other characters might perceive as flaws.

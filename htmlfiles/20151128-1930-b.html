When an ice cube is floating in water, the total mass of the ice cube is equal to the total mass that the water would have if it were occupying the space taken up by the submerged portion of the ice cube - this is needed in order for the buoyancy force on the ice cube to equal its weight. The ice is of lower density, yes, so this is why an ice cube floats, with some portion above the water.

If the ice cube melts, then, the resulting water will take up the same volume as the portion of the ice cube that was underwater before it melts. Hence, the water level will not rise or fall - it will stay the same.

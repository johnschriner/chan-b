it's Japanese chess. Main differences:

1) there is no black or white, the direction of the pieces indicate the owner.
2) at every turn, a play may choose to either move a piece on the board, or to reintroduce a previously captured piece. This is called a 'drop'.
3) piece may be dropped at any location on the board, except when that would mean the piece would not be able to move (e.g. pawn on last row).
4) there are only two long-range pieces for each player, one rook and one bishop, and all pieces are severely constrained in movement, especially backwards.
5) unlike chess, shogi games can often go from opening to mid-game to end-game, and then back into mid-game as attacks on the opposing king are deflected or fizzle out. Stalemates are also much more common in shogi.

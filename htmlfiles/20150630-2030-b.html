The initial upward velocity of this dude is sin(15) * 10 m/s, which is around 2.6 m/s. His horizontal velocity is cos(15) * 1, so he travels about 9.6 meters per second.

Given these numbers, we need to find the intersection between the downward sloping plane and the parabola that describes his path of motion.

The parabola can first be described by parametric equations, in terms of our x and y. His x position can be determined at any point in time t by simply calculating 9.6*t = x. His y position is described by a slightly more complex equation y = 2.6*t - 9.8t^2. If we bring these together, y = 2.6*(x/9.6) - 9.8(x/9.6)^2.

Now, let us discuss the downward slope. Because we have a velocity and the angle of the platform, we can determine the slope quite easily. Knowing that it slopes at 50 degrees, we can determine that for every 9.6 m traveled horizontally, it travels downward 11.4 m. This gives us a slope of -11.4/9.6, and a linear equation of y = -11.4/9.6 * x describing the line.

Now we simply need to find the intersection of these two equations. I'll leave this as an exercise for the reader. Our end result is a distance of 13.7m traveled before he hits the ramp.
